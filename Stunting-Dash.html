Data Analysis - Stunting Case in Indonesia

The analysis and the dashboard below will show how the relationship between IDM status in a district and the stunting rate in that area is related. Through the dashboard, you can see the overall stunting rate in Indonesia through a Geo Map and can filter data by district, province or year.

Goal

Asking the right questions and analyzing the stunting data and IDM data Indonesia to give relevant insights. The goal is to help the government, to collaborate with local governments in formulating strategies to reduce stunting rates in Indonesia.

Result

Results are presented in a report with data dashboard. From analysis there are recommendations and follow-up steps. Based on that, clear and well-founded decisions also right strategies can be made to reduced stunting rates in Indonesia.

Project Duration

Project duration of this is 5 weeks from 29 May 2022 until 3 July 2022 from collect data until make a report to gain insight. In order to carry out the project as quickly as possible, it is important that the relevant data is available, complete and clean.

Case : Are the number of IDM have an affect on stunting rates in Indonesia?

Based on SSGI (Study on Nutritional Status of Indonesia) in 2021 the stunting rate nationally reached 24.4% (Ministry of Health), above the standard by WHO, which is 20% (WHO). In 2021, the IDM (Indeks Desa Membangun) or building village Index recorded that villages with "backward" or tertinggal status have a percentage of 16.49% (12177 villages) based in 3 indicators that is IKE (Economic Conditions Index includes logistics access, market access, access banking ), IKL(Environtmental Sensitivity Index includes environmental quality, natural disasters, disaster response) and IKS(Healthy Family Index includes Health, Education, sosial capital, settlement) (Kemendesa). The dataset used in this analysis includes the stunting case dataset in 2013 and 2018 and IDM data in 2016 and 2018 from stunting.go.id and IDM Kemendesa web page.

How distribution of stunting rates in Indonesia?

From the graph map, it can be seen that stunting rates in almost all regions in Indonesia are still very high at above 20% and it can be seen that the Timor Tengah Selatan district in NTT has the highest average stunting rates, which is 63.22%.

What are the stunting rates in 2013 and 2018?

Because the number of stunting rates is still high, based on the data obtained, a comparison was made between the stunting rates in 2013 and 2018 in all regions. From the graph, it can be seen that the overall prevalence or stunting rate has decreased from 2013 to 2018 but the stunting rate overall is still above 20%.

How is the number of districts based on IDM status?

The chart shows that the number of regions with IDM backward status have quite high stunting cases compared to regions with growing or advanced IDM status. And it can be seen from the pie chart that the percentage of regions with backward status is quite high with a percentage of 48.6%.

How is the correlation between IDM status and stunting rates?

The IDM and stunting rates have negative correlation.


    What:
  • It can be seen that the IDM and stunting rates have a negative correlation. This means that there is an inverse relationship between the two. If the IDM value in an area is high then the stunting rates in that area tend to be low and vice versa if the IDM value in an area is low then the stunting rates in that area tend to be high.
  • From the graph it can also be seen that the IKL (Environtmental Sensitivity Index) parameter has a higher correlation value than other parameters in the IDM.

    Why:
  • If viewed based on Environmental Statistics in Indonesia in 2018, there was an increase in emissions from industrial liquid waste, and since 2016 the composition of industrial liquid waste emissions is greater than domestic solid waste. Emissions generated from industrial liquid waste are 43% and domestic liquid waste are 18%, the rest is from domestic solid waste.
  • Based on Environmental Statistics in Indonesia also recorded that water pollution occurred in 25.1% of villages and 2.7% of land pollution, which then as many as 28% of households did not have access to clean water which in the end also resulted in health problems which were estimated at 7 million cases of diarrhea with 39.6%. the case is not handled.
Source here

    Actions/Solutions:
  • The government needs to increase the IDM value in each region or district, especially areas with low IDM scores.
  • The government needs to improve the quality of the environment in each area such as providing clear regulations for industrial companies to manage the waste they produce properly, and also providing good waste management facilities for the community so as not to pollute the environment.
